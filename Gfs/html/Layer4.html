Vocab you have to know:

    Voc.:

  1. segments = refer to small, discrete units of data that are transmitted over a network
  2. reliability = refers to the ability of a computer to perform its intended functions consistently, accurately, and without errors or failures.
  3. flow control = refers to the process of managing the rate of data transmission between devices on a network to prevent data loss, congestion, and other issues
  4. acknowledgments = receiving device to confirm that it has successfully received a packet or segment of data transmitted by a sender
  5. connection-oriented = refers to a type of communication protocol in which a reliable and dedicated connection is established between two devices before data is transmitted
  6. encapsulation = refers to the process of enclosing data in a specific format or protocol to enable its transmission over a network
  7. conseptual = refers to something that exists in the mind or as an abstract idea rather than a physical or concrete entity

Fourth Layer The transport layer facilitates the transfer
of variable-length data sequences from a source host to a destination host across a network,
while ensuring quality-of-service functions.
This involves breaking down large protocol data units or long data streams into smaller segments
due to the maximum packet size imposed by the network layer,
called the maximum transmission unit (MTU).
The amount of data in a segment must be small enough to allow for a network-layer header
and a transport-layer header. The transport layer controls the reliability
of a given link between the source and destination hosts through flow control,
error control, and acknowledgments of sequence and existence.
Some protocols are state- and connection-oriented,
allowing the transport layer to keep track of segments and retransmit those that fail delivery.
However, protocols like UDP are used in applications that can tolerate some packet loss,
reordering, errors, or duplication.
The OSI connection-oriented transport protocol defines five classes
of connection-mode transport protocols ranging from TP0 to TP4,
with TP0 providing the fewest features and TP4 being the most reliable.
The transport layer can be compared to a post office that deals with the dispatch
and classification of mail and parcels sent.
Tunnelling protocols operate at the transport layer,
such as carrying non-IP protocols or end-to-end encryption with IPsec.
While GRE might seem like a network-layer protocol,
it becomes closer to a transport protocol when encapsulation of the payload takes place only
at the endpoint. L2TP carries PPP frames inside transport segments.
