The Physical layer is the first layer of the OSI model.
It deals with the physical transmission of data over the network,
including the physical components of the network such as cables,
connectors, and network interface cards. The Physical layer defines the characteristics
of the physical medium, including the voltage levels, timing, and data rate.
This layer also defines how data is transmitted over the medium,
including the modulation and demodulation techniques used to encode and decode the data.
The Physical layer is responsible for converting digital data into a format that can be transmitted
over the network and vice versa.
